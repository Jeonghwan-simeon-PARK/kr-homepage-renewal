Privacy Policy

Effective Date: January 1, 2026

Last Updated: March 15, 2026

HicareNet, Inc. ("HicareNet," "we," "us," or "our"), a California-based subsidiary of Hicare Co., Ltd., is committed to protecting the privacy and security of your personal information. This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our Remote Patient Monitoring (RPM), Chronic Care Management (CCM), Annual Wellness Visit (AWV), and Value-Based Care (VBC) services, or visit our website.

1. Information We Collect

Personal Information

We may collect the following personal information when you interact with our services:

  • Name, address, email address, and phone number
  • Date of birth, gender, and other demographic information
  • Insurance and billing information
  • Professional credentials and organizational affiliations (for healthcare providers)

Protected Health Information (PHI)

In the course of providing our healthcare IT services, we may process:

  • Vital signs and biometric data collected through RPM devices (blood pressure, glucose levels, weight, pulse oximetry, temperature)
  • Chronic condition management records and care plans
  • Clinical notes, diagnoses, and treatment information
  • Medication and prescription data

Usage and Technical Data

We automatically collect certain information when you visit our website or use our platform:

  • IP address, browser type, operating system, and device identifiers
  • Pages visited, time spent, and navigation patterns
  • Referring URLs and search terms
  • Log data and error reports for service improvement

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • Providing and managing Remote Patient Monitoring and Chronic Care Management services
  • Facilitating communication between patients and healthcare providers
  • Processing billing, insurance claims, and payment transactions
  • Improving our platform, services, and user experience through analytics
  • Complying with legal obligations, including HIPAA and state healthcare regulations
  • Sending service-related notifications and updates
  • Conducting quality assurance and clinical outcome analysis
  • Responding to inquiries, support requests, and feedback

3. HIPAA Compliance

As a healthcare technology provider, HicareNet is committed to full compliance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) and the HITECH Act.

PHI Handling

All Protected Health Information processed through our platform is handled in strict accordance with HIPAA Privacy and Security Rules. We implement administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of PHI.

Business Associate Agreements

We enter into Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) with all covered entities and subcontractors who handle PHI through our platform. These agreements define the permitted uses and disclosures of PHI and establish obligations for safeguarding health information.

Minimum Necessary Standard

We apply the minimum necessary standard to all uses, disclosures, and requests for PHI. Access to PHI is limited to authorized personnel who require it to perform their designated functions, and is controlled through role-based access controls.

4. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell your personal information. We may share your information only in the following circumstances:

  • Healthcare Providers: To facilitate treatment, care coordination, and clinical operations as authorized
  • Service Providers: With trusted third-party vendors who assist in operating our platform, subject to confidentiality obligations and BAAs where applicable
  • Legal Requirements: When required by law, regulation, legal process, or governmental request
  • Business Transfers: In connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, with appropriate safeguards
  • With Consent: When you have provided explicit authorization for a specific disclosure

5. Data Security

We employ comprehensive security measures to protect your information:

  • Encryption: All data in transit is protected with TLS 1.2 or higher. Data at rest is encrypted using AES-256 encryption
  • ISO 27001 Certification: Our information security management system is certified to the ISO 27001 international standard
  • ISO 27701 Certification: Our privacy information management system meets the ISO 27701 standard for data privacy
  • Access Controls: Multi-factor authentication, role-based access, and regular access reviews
  • Monitoring: Continuous security monitoring, vulnerability assessments, and regular penetration testing
  • Incident Response: Documented breach notification procedures in compliance with HIPAA and applicable state laws

6. Your Rights

You have the following rights regarding your personal information and PHI:

  • Right to Access: You may request a copy of the personal information we hold about you
  • Right to Correction: You may request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information
  • Right to Deletion: You may request deletion of your personal information, subject to legal retention requirements
  • Right to Restriction: You may request that we restrict certain processing of your information
  • Right to an Accounting of Disclosures: You may request a record of certain disclosures of your PHI
  • Right to Opt-Out: You may opt out of non-essential communications at any time

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at info@hicare.net. We will respond to your request within 30 days as required by applicable law.

7. Cookie Policy

Our website uses cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your experience:

  • Essential Cookies: Required for basic website functionality, including authentication and security
  • Analytics Cookies: Help us understand how visitors interact with our website to improve performance
  • Preference Cookies: Remember your language selection and other display preferences

You can manage cookie preferences through your browser settings. Disabling certain cookies may limit your ability to use some features of our website.

8. Data Retention

We retain personal information and PHI for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this Privacy Policy, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements. Healthcare records are retained in accordance with HIPAA requirements and applicable state medical record retention laws. When data is no longer needed, it is securely deleted or de-identified.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology, legal requirements, or other factors. When we make material changes, we will update the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page and, where required, notify you by email or through a prominent notice on our website. We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically.
