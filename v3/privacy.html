Privacy Policy

Effective Date: January 1, 2025 · Last Updated: March 15, 2025

1. Information We Collect

HicareNet, Inc. ("HicareNet," "we," "us," or "our"), a US subsidiary of Hicare Co., Ltd., collects information necessary to deliver our healthcare technology services. The types of information we collect include:

Personal Information

  • Name, email address, phone number, and mailing address
  • Professional credentials and organizational affiliation
  • Account login credentials and authentication data
  • Billing and payment information

Protected Health Information (PHI)

  • Patient demographic information as required for Remote Patient Monitoring (RPM) and Chronic Care Management (CCM) services
  • Clinical data including vital signs, biometric readings, and health assessments
  • Annual Wellness Visit (AWV) assessment data and care plan information
  • Treatment records and care coordination communications

Usage and Technical Data

  • Device information, browser type, and operating system
  • IP address and approximate geographic location
  • Platform usage patterns, feature interactions, and session duration
  • Error logs and performance diagnostics

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

Service Delivery

  • Providing and maintaining our RPM, CCM, AWV, and Value-Based Care platform services
  • Processing and transmitting clinical data between healthcare providers and patients
  • Generating care plans, clinical reports, and compliance documentation
  • Facilitating FHIR-based EMR integrations and health information exchange

Service Improvement

  • Analyzing aggregated, de-identified data to improve platform performance and reliability
  • Developing new features and enhancing existing clinical workflows
  • Conducting quality assurance testing and security audits

Communication

  • Sending service-related notifications, system alerts, and account updates
  • Responding to support requests and inquiries
  • Providing regulatory updates and compliance notifications relevant to your organization

3. HIPAA Compliance

As a healthcare technology provider, HicareNet operates in full compliance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) and the HITECH Act.

PHI Handling

All Protected Health Information processed through our platform is handled in accordance with HIPAA Privacy, Security, and Breach Notification Rules. We implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of PHI.

Business Associate Agreements

HicareNet enters into Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) with all covered entities and their business associates before accessing or processing any PHI. Our BAA is based on the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services model agreement and is reviewed annually.

Minimum Necessary Standard

We adhere to the HIPAA Minimum Necessary Standard, limiting the use and disclosure of PHI to the minimum amount reasonably necessary to accomplish the intended purpose. Role-based access controls enforce this principle across all systems and personnel.

4. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell your personal information or PHI. We may share information in the following circumstances:

  • Healthcare Providers: With authorized healthcare providers and care team members as necessary for treatment, payment, and healthcare operations
  • Service Providers: With vetted third-party service providers who are bound by BAAs and confidentiality obligations and who assist in operating our platform
  • Legal Requirements: When required by applicable law, court order, subpoena, or government regulation
  • Business Transfers: In connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, with appropriate privacy protections maintained
  • With Consent: With your explicit written authorization for any purpose not described in this policy

5. Data Security

HicareNet implements comprehensive security measures to protect your information, backed by internationally recognized certifications:

  • Encryption: AES-256 encryption for data at rest and TLS 1.3 for data in transit, with regularly rotated encryption keys
  • Access Controls: Role-based access control (RBAC) with mandatory multi-factor authentication for all system access
  • ISO 27001 Certification: Our Information Security Management System is independently audited and certified to ISO 27001 standards
  • ISO 27701 Certification: Our Privacy Information Management System extends ISO 27001 with specific privacy controls for PII protection
  • Monitoring: 24/7 security monitoring with intrusion detection systems, SIEM integration, and automated alerting
  • Incident Response: Documented incident response plan with 24-hour breach notification commitment to affected parties

6. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction and applicable law (including HIPAA and the California Consumer Privacy Act), you may have the following rights regarding your information:

  • Access: Request a copy of the personal information and PHI we hold about you
  • Correction: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal information
  • Deletion: Request deletion of your personal information, subject to legal retention requirements and exceptions
  • Data Portability: Receive your data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format
  • Restriction: Request restriction of processing of your personal information under certain circumstances
  • Accounting of Disclosures: Request an accounting of certain disclosures of your PHI as provided under HIPAA

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the information provided in the Contact Information section below. We will respond to verified requests within 30 days, or as required by applicable law.

7. Cookie Policy

Our website and platform use cookies and similar technologies to enhance your experience and collect usage data.

Types of Cookies We Use

  • Essential Cookies: Required for core platform functionality, authentication, and security. These cannot be disabled.
  • Performance Cookies: Collect anonymized data about how visitors use our website, helping us improve site performance and user experience.
  • Functional Cookies: Remember your preferences such as language selection and display settings.

Managing Cookies

You can manage cookie preferences through your browser settings. Please note that disabling essential cookies may affect platform functionality. We do not use advertising or tracking cookies.

8. Data Retention

We retain personal information and PHI for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this policy, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements. Healthcare records are retained in accordance with applicable federal and state retention requirements. When data is no longer required, it is securely destroyed using methods appropriate to the sensitivity of the information.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically to reflect changes in our practices, technologies, legal requirements, or other factors. When we make material changes, we will notify you by posting the updated policy on our website with a revised "Last Updated" date and, where required, provide additional notice through email or in-platform notification. We encourage you to review this policy regularly.
