Privacy Policy

Effective Date: January 1, 2025 · Last Updated: March 15, 2025

HicareNet Co., Ltd. ("HicareNet," "we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting the privacy and security of your personal information. This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard information when you visit our website, use our remote patient monitoring (RPM) platform, chronic care management (CCM) services, or any of our connected care solutions.

1. Information We Collect

Personal Information

We may collect the following personal information when you interact with our services:

  • Full name, email address, phone number, and mailing address
  • Professional credentials and organizational affiliation
  • Account login credentials (username and hashed password)
  • Billing and payment information
  • Communication preferences and correspondence history

Protected Health Information (PHI)

In the course of providing our healthcare services, we may collect and process PHI as defined under HIPAA, including:

  • Patient demographic information (name, date of birth, contact details)
  • Medical record numbers and health plan identifiers
  • Vital signs and biometric data collected through RPM devices (blood pressure, blood glucose, pulse oximetry, weight, temperature)
  • Clinical notes, care plans, and treatment histories
  • Medication lists and allergy information
  • Insurance and claims data

Usage Data

We automatically collect certain information when you access our platform:

  • IP address, browser type, operating system, and device identifiers
  • Pages visited, features used, and time spent on the platform
  • Referring URLs and search terms
  • Error logs and performance data

2. How We Use Your Information

Service Delivery

We use the information we collect to:

  • Provide, operate, and maintain our RPM, CCM, AWV, and Value-Based Care solutions
  • Process and transmit patient health data between monitoring devices, our platform, and authorized healthcare providers
  • Generate clinical alerts, reports, and care management workflows
  • Facilitate FHIR-based EMR integration and health information exchange

Improvement

  • Analyze usage patterns to improve platform functionality and user experience
  • Develop new features and services based on aggregated, de-identified data
  • Conduct quality assurance and performance optimization

Communication

  • Respond to inquiries, support requests, and demo scheduling
  • Send service-related notifications, updates, and security alerts
  • Provide educational content and healthcare industry insights (with consent)

3. HIPAA Compliance

PHI Handling

HicareNet acts as a Business Associate under HIPAA when processing Protected Health Information on behalf of Covered Entities (healthcare providers). All PHI is handled in strict accordance with the HIPAA Privacy Rule (45 CFR Part 160 and Subparts A and E of Part 164) and the HIPAA Security Rule (45 CFR Part 160 and Subparts A and C of Part 164).

Business Associate Agreements (BAAs)

We enter into Business Associate Agreements with all Covered Entities before accessing, processing, or storing any PHI. Our BAAs define the permitted uses and disclosures of PHI, our obligations to safeguard the data, and breach notification procedures.

Minimum Necessary Standard

We apply the Minimum Necessary Standard to all uses and disclosures of PHI, ensuring that only the minimum amount of information necessary to accomplish the intended purpose is accessed or shared.

4. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell your personal information or PHI. We may share information in the following circumstances:

  • Healthcare Providers: PHI is shared with authorized healthcare providers and care team members as necessary for treatment, payment, and healthcare operations.
  • Service Providers: We engage trusted third-party service providers who assist in operating our platform (e.g., cloud hosting, data analytics). These providers are bound by contractual obligations and, where applicable, Business Associate Agreements.
  • Legal Requirements: We may disclose information when required by law, regulation, court order, or governmental request, including compliance with HIPAA breach notification requirements.
  • Business Transfers: In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction, subject to the same privacy protections.
  • With Consent: We may share information for purposes not described in this policy with your explicit consent.

5. Data Security

HicareNet maintains a comprehensive information security program certified to international standards:

  • ISO 27001:2022 Certified: Our Information Security Management System (ISMS) is independently audited and certified, demonstrating systematic management of information security risks.
  • ISO 27701:2019 Certified: Our Privacy Information Management System (PIMS) extends our ISMS to include privacy-specific controls aligned with GDPR, CCPA, and other global privacy regulations.
  • AES-256 Encryption: All data is encrypted at rest using AES-256 encryption and in transit using TLS 1.3 protocols.
  • Role-Based Access Control (RBAC): Access to PHI and sensitive data is restricted based on job function, with multi-factor authentication (MFA) required for all administrative access.
  • 24/7 Security Monitoring: Our Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) system provides continuous monitoring, threat detection, and automated incident response.
  • Regular Assessments: We conduct quarterly penetration testing, annual security risk assessments, and continuous vulnerability scanning.

6. Your Rights

Depending on your relationship with HicareNet and applicable law, you may have the following rights:

  • Access: Request access to the personal information and PHI we hold about you.
  • Correction: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Deletion: Request deletion of your personal information, subject to legal retention requirements and HIPAA obligations.
  • Portability: Request a copy of your data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format.
  • Restriction: Request restrictions on certain processing activities.
  • Accounting of Disclosures: Under HIPAA, patients have the right to request an accounting of certain disclosures of their PHI made by HicareNet.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the information provided in Section 10 below.

7. Cookie Policy

Our website uses cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your experience:

Essential Cookies

Required for the website to function properly. These cookies enable core functionality such as security, session management, and accessibility preferences. They cannot be disabled.

Performance Cookies

Help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting anonymous usage statistics. This data is used to improve site performance and user experience. These cookies can be disabled through your browser settings.

Functional Cookies

Enable enhanced functionality and personalization, such as language preferences, region selection, and saved form data. Disabling these cookies may affect certain features of the website.

You can manage your cookie preferences through your browser settings. Please note that disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of our website.

8. Data Retention

We retain personal information and PHI for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this policy, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements. Specific retention periods include:

  • PHI is retained in accordance with HIPAA requirements and applicable state medical record retention laws (minimum 6 years from date of creation or last effective date).
  • Account information is retained for the duration of the business relationship plus 3 years.
  • Usage data is retained for up to 2 years in identifiable form and may be retained indefinitely in aggregated, de-identified form.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology, legal requirements, or other factors. When we make material changes, we will notify you by posting the updated policy on our website with a revised "Last Updated" date, and where required by law, we will provide additional notice (such as email notification).

We encourage you to review this policy periodically to stay informed about how we protect your information.
