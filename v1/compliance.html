Technical Safeguards

Data Security Practices

Multi-layered security controls protect your data at every level of our infrastructure.

Encryption

AES-256 encryption at rest and TLS 1.3 for all data in transit. All PHI is encrypted end-to-end with regularly rotated keys.

Access Control

Role-based access control (RBAC) with mandatory multi-factor authentication. Comprehensive audit logs track all data access events.

Infrastructure

Hosted on HIPAA-eligible cloud infrastructure with SOC 2 Type II compliance. Physically secure data centers with 99.99% uptime SLA.

Monitoring

24/7 security monitoring with SIEM integration and automated intrusion detection. Real-time alerting for anomalous activity patterns.

Backup & Recovery

Automated daily backups with geo-redundant storage across multiple availability zones. Point-in-time recovery with tested disaster recovery plans.

Incident Response

Documented incident response plan with defined escalation paths. 24-hour breach notification commitment and regular tabletop exercises.

Common Questions

Compliance FAQ

Can HicareNet sign a Business Associate Agreement (BAA)?

Yes. HicareNet provides a signed Business Associate Agreement to all covered entities and their business associates. Our BAA is based on the HHS model agreement and is reviewed annually by our legal and compliance teams to ensure it reflects current regulatory requirements.

Can we review HicareNet's audit reports?

Yes. Upon signing an NDA, we can share our ISO 27001 and ISO 27701 certification reports, SOC 2 Type II report, and our most recent penetration test summary. Contact our compliance team to request access to these documents.

Where is patient data stored?

All patient data is stored within the United States on HIPAA-eligible cloud infrastructure. We use geographically redundant data centers to ensure availability and disaster recovery. Data never leaves US jurisdiction, and we provide data residency documentation upon request.

How does HicareNet handle data breach notification?

HicareNet follows a documented breach notification procedure compliant with HIPAA and state breach notification laws. In the event of a confirmed breach involving PHI, we commit to notifying affected covered entities within 24 hours of discovery. Our incident response team conducts a thorough investigation and provides a detailed report of the incident, impact, and remediation steps taken.
